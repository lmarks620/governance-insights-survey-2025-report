Executive summary

358 board leaders (directors, CEOs, executive directors, corporate secretaries, board administrators) completed the Governance Insights 2025 survey. Key takeaways:

  • Maturity is mixed: One-third are “Developing” (periodic reviews, insights not always timely or acted on); about one-quarter each are “Basic” or “Advanced.” A clear majority expect to be in “Developing” or “Advanced” in 12 months.
  • AI interest is strong: 72% are somewhat to very interested (3–5 on a 5-point scale) in using AI-generated governance insights. 8% are already using AI today; 33% have moderate expectation to use it within 12 months.
  • Biggest barrier: “Exists but fragmented/manual” (195 mentions)—insights spread across people and files, requiring manual effort. “No owner/follow-through” and “Not actionable” are also top pain points.
  • Trust matters for AI: Strong data privacy, human review before sharing, and transparent audit trails are the top requirements for AI-generated insights.
  • Visibility varies by role: Corporate Secretary/General Counsel report the highest ease accessing insights (e.g., materials, oversight/risk). Executive Directors report the lowest ease on director engagement—a clear persona slice for product.

What boards want from AI (trust & controls)

Respondents selected which capabilities are important for AI-generated insights.

Maturity of AI insights: Interest is high (72% at 3–5) but only 8% “Already using AI” today. Expectation is mostly “Moderate” (33%) or “No/Low” (45%). This signals a now window for product: meet trust requirements (privacy, audit trail, human review) and deliver clear use cases (e.g., follow-through, engagement) to capture the “moderate expectation” segment.

Barriers to governance insights

What gets in the way of boards having the insights they need (multiple selections allowed).

Product angle: “Fragmented/manual” and “No owner/follow-through” point to workflow and ownership. “Not actionable” and “Hard to locate” point to clarity and discoverability. AI and better tooling can target consolidation, clear ownership, and actionable next steps.

Persona & industry slices

By role

Board Director/Member (49%), Board Administrator (15%), Corporate Secretary/General Counsel (12%), Executive Director (9%), CEO/President (9%), Other (6%).

By industry

Nonprofit leads (49%), then Education (17%), Financial services (11%), Healthcare (7%), Corporate (7%), Other (6%), Government (2%).

Visibility / access by persona: Ease of accessing insights varies by role. Corporate Secretary/General Counsel report the highest “Easy + Very Easy” on materials (61%) and oversight/risk (45%). Executive Directors report the lowest on director engagement (28%). Financial services and Corporate show relatively higher “very interested” (5) in AI—worth slicing in GTM and product.

Cross-question distinctions

  • Maturity → AI expectation: Boards that are already “Advanced” today are much more likely to be “Already using AI” (19 of 89) or “High expectation” (8). “Basic” and “Informal” skew toward “No expectation” or “Low expectation”—suggesting product and GTM should segment by maturity.
  • Role → AI interest: Corporate Secretary/General Counsel and Board Director/Member show the highest share at 4–5 (very interested). “Other” role has the highest share at 1 (not interested)—worth understanding who falls in “Other” for targeting.
  • Industry → AI interest: Nonprofit is the largest segment and spread across the interest scale; Financial services and Corporate have a higher proportion at 5 (very interested), so AI messaging may resonate more there first.

Product direction: Now / Next / Later

Now

Double down on follow-through and ownership: action tracking, due dates, status. Address “fragmented/manual” and “no owner/follow-through” with clear workflows and visibility.

Next

AI-generated insights with trust first: strong privacy, human review, transparent audit trail, clear citations. Prioritize use cases: director engagement, follow-through, quality of materials.

Later

Structured reporting across governance areas (strategy, risk, composition) and early warning on risks/compliance. Deeper persona-specific views (e.g., engagement for EDs, materials for Secretaries).
